# Miracle Center Youth Mentorship Program
## 8-Week Mentor Helper Guide
### Theme: Identity — *Who am I in God?*
#### Theme Verse: 1 Peter 2:9
*"But you are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that you may proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of darkness into His marvelous light."*

---

## How to Use This Guide

This guide is a weekly companion for mentors. It is designed to work alongside whatever Bible character your mentee has chosen to study. Each week builds on a specific dimension of identity in Christ, and your role as mentor is to help the mentee discover that same truth **in the life of their character — and in their own life**.

**Before each meeting:**
- Pray for your mentee by name.
- Review the week's theme and questions.
- Read the key verse and sit with it personally first.

**During each meeting:**
- Open in prayer (2-3 minutes).
- Check in on life and heart (5 minutes).
- Walk through the weekly questions (30-40 minutes).
- Share the exhortation and speak the proclamation together.
- Close with prayer, covering the mentee's specific requests.

**You do not need all the answers. You need presence, prayer, and the Word.**

---

## Before Week 1 — Mentee Preparation Assignment

Ask your mentee to come to the first session ready to share:

1. **Which Bible character did you choose, and why?**
2. What is one thing you already know about this person?
3. What is one question you have about their life?

*The character they choose is not random — trust the Holy Spirit in their selection.*

# WEEK 1 — You Were Known Before You Were Born
## Theme: Origin — Before the world said who you are, God already knew

### Memory Verse
> *"Before I formed you in the womb I knew you; before you were born I set you apart."*
> — **Jeremiah 1:5**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
Identity confusion begins when we let the world name us before we know what God already said. This first week, you are planting a foundation stone: God knew your mentee before any experience, failure, or label could touch them. Your job is to help them feel the weight and the glory of being **known by God first**.

In a culture addicted to self-discovery, the gospel declares that identity is not found — it is received.

---

### Conversation Questions
*Tie each question to the mentee's chosen Bible character as you go.*

1. **Opening check-in:** How are you feeling about starting this program? What do you hope changes in you by the end?
2. When people ask *"Who are you?"*, what is the first thing that comes to mind for you? Why?
3. What labels have others put on you — positive or negative — that you have started to believe about yourself?
4. Looking at your Bible character: What was their background, family, or situation before God called them? What did the world see when they looked at this person?
5. Is there a moment in your character's story where God showed He knew this person long before others recognized them? What does that tell you about how God sees *you*?
6. If you could hear God speak one sentence over your identity today, what would you want Him to say?

---

### Weekly Proclamation
*Speak this aloud together at the end of the meeting:*

> **"I was known by God before anyone named me. I was chosen before I could earn it. My identity begins with Him — not with the world, not with my past, not with my mistakes. I am already known. I am already loved. I am already His."**

---

- Is there anything in your past or current situation you need God to speak truth over?

# WEEK 2 — Made on Purpose, Not by Accident
## Theme: Design — You were crafted with intention

### Memory Verse
> *"For you created my inmost being; you knit me together in my mother's womb. I praise you because I am fearfully and wonderfully made."*
> — **Psalm 139:13-14**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
Many young people secretly believe they are a mistake — not dramatic enough, not gifted enough, the wrong skin, the wrong family, the wrong story. This week confronts that lie at the root.

Psalm 139 is not a compliment. It is a declaration of craftsmanship. God *knit* your mentee together — every detail, every personality trait, every part of the way they are wired is intentional. Help them receive that this week.

The enemy attacks design to destroy assignment. When a young person hates what God made, they resist the very tools God gave them for purpose.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. What is one thing about yourself you genuinely like? (Mentor: affirm it as God's design.)
2. What is one thing about yourself you have struggled to accept or have been insecure about?
3. How was your Bible character uniquely designed for what God called them to? What traits, background, or even struggles equipped them?
4. Did your character ever doubt or resist who they were? What did God say or do in response?
5. Looking at how God designed your character — what might that say about how God is designing *you* for something specific?
6. What would change in your daily life if you truly believed God crafted you on purpose?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"I am not a mistake. I am not an accident. Every part of me — my personality, my story, my gifts, even my struggles — was crafted by the hands of God. I am fearfully and wonderfully made. I will not despise what God designed. I receive my design as holy ground."**

---

### Prayer Space

*Ask your mentee:*
- Is there a specific part of yourself you have rejected or resented that you want to bring before God?
- Who in your life right now do you need God to help you see through His eyes (including yourself)?

# WEEK 3 — You Belong to Someone
## Theme: Belonging — Identity is rooted in relationship, not performance

### Memory Verse
> *"See what great love the Father has lavished on us, that we should be called children of God! And that is what we are!"*
> — **1 John 3:1**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
Belonging is one of the deepest human needs — and one of the enemy's primary targets in this generation. Social media manufactures belonging while stealing it. Peer pressure exists because everyone is searching for a tribe.

This week, anchor your mentee in the most secure belonging available: **sonship and daughtership in God.** Not earned. Not based on performance. Lavished.

Your presence as a mentor is itself a living illustration of this truth. You chose to show up for them. That is what our Father does.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. Where do you feel most like you belong right now? (family, friend group, church, team, etc.)
2. Have you ever felt like an outsider — somewhere or in general? How did that feel?
3. Your Bible character — where did they find belonging? Who were their people, and who rejected or isolated them?
4. Was there a moment where God became their source of belonging rather than a person or group? What shifted in them?
5. What does it mean to you personally that God calls you His child — not His servant, not His project, but His child?
6. How should knowing you belong to God change how you handle rejection, loneliness, or the pressure to fit in?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"I belong to God. I am not searching for a tribe — I am already part of a royal family. No rejection, no loneliness, and no social pressure can take away what the Father has placed in me. I am His child. That is settled. I walk in that every day."**

---

### Prayer Space

*Ask your mentee:*
- Is there a relationship, rejection, or loneliness you want God to meet you in this week?
- Is there someone you feel pressure to impress or belong to that is competing with your identity in God?

# WEEK 4 — You Have a Name and an Assignment
## Theme: Purpose — Identity leads to calling

### Memory Verse
> *"For we are God's handiwork, created in Christ Jesus to do good works, which God prepared in advance for us to do."*
> — **Ephesians 2:10**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
Identity without purpose is incomplete. God does not just know us — He assigns us. One Peter 2:9 says we are chosen *"that we may proclaim the praises of Him who called us."* Belonging comes with a send.

This week move your mentee from being a receiver of identity to also becoming a carrier of purpose. Their Bible character was not just special — they were **sent**. Help them see that the same is true for them.

Be careful not to rush to big "call to ministry" language. Assignment often starts small: showing up for a sibling, being honest at school, serving at church. Purpose is lived before it is announced.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. If someone asked you *"What were you made to do?"*, what would you say?
2. What activities, moments, or situations make you feel most alive and fully yourself?
3. What was the specific assignment God gave your Bible character? Did they understand it immediately, or did it unfold over time?
4. How did their identity shape their assignment? Could someone else have fulfilled the same purpose differently because of their different design?
5. Is there something — a dream, an idea, a call — that you keep sensing in your heart but have been afraid to say out loud?
6. What is one small thing you can do *this week* that aligns with who God is making you to be?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"I was not put here by accident. God prepared works for me before I was born. My life is not random — it is a mission. I choose to stop waiting to become someone and start walking in who I already am. My assignment is already in motion. I will not shrink back."**

---

### Prayer Space

*Ask your mentee:*
- Is there something God is calling you toward that you have been procrastinating or afraid of?
- How can I pray specifically for your sense of purpose and direction this week?

# WEEK 5 — Who You Are When No One Is Watching
## Theme: Character — Identity lived in private shapes testimony in public

### Memory Verse
> *"Do not conform to the pattern of this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind. Then you will be able to test and approve what God's will is — his good, pleasing and perfect will."*
> — **Romans 12:2**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
By week 5 your mentee is mid-program. This is often when the initial excitement fades and the real work of identity begins. Character is what happens when no one is applauding.

This week get honest. Ask hard questions in love. Your Bible character likely had private moments — temptations, failures, hidden choices — that shaped who they became in public. Help your mentee connect private disciplines to public character.

Revival does not begin with movement; it begins with individuals who choose holiness in private. Be honest with your mentee about your own journey in this.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. Honest check-in: How is your private life — what you think, what you consume, what you do when no one is watching — compared to who you want to be publicly?
2. Is there a gap between who you are with your mentor and with your church friends versus who you are at school, online, or at home?
3. What was your Bible character like in private moments? Was there a failure or hidden struggle in their story? What happened as a result?
4. How did God respond to your character's private failures — with punishment, with restoration, or both? What does this say about grace and accountability?
5. What is one private habit — spiritual or otherwise — that you sense God wants to transform right now?
6. What does a renewed mind actually look like in your daily routine?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"I choose to become the same person in every room. God sees the private, and I invite Him into it. I will not perform faith publicly while hiding compromise privately. I submit my mind, my habits, and my hidden places to transformation by the Holy Spirit. I am being renewed."**

---

### Prayer Space

*Ask your mentee:*
- Is there something private you would like to confess or bring to God today — you can be as specific or as general as you need?
- What habit or pattern do you want God to break or build this week?

# WEEK 6 — You Can Overcome What Tried to Define You
## Theme: Redemption — Past wounds do not have the final word on identity

### Memory Verse
> *"And we know that in all things God works for the good of those who love him, who have been called according to his purpose."*
> — **Romans 8:28**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
Week 6 is a holy week. If you have built trust well, your mentee may open doors they have never opened before. Be ready to receive what they carry.

Every young person carries *something*: a wound, a shame, a family failure, a personal sin they have not fully released. This week is about redemption — not pretending it did not happen, but allowing God to work it for good.

Your Bible character almost certainly had a wound, a failure, or an experience that the enemy tried to use to lock their identity in the past. God refused to let that be the final word. Model that posture as you minister.

This week: offer more presence than answers. Ask God to show up.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. Is there something in your past — something done to you or something you did — that still feels like it defines you in some way?
2. What do you wish people did not know about you? (You do not need to share it — just sit with the question and bring it to God.)
3. In your Bible character's story — what was the wound, failure, or season that could have ended their story? How did God redeem it?
4. Is there anything in their past that actually **became** part of their testimony and ministry? How does that make you feel about your own?
5. What would it look like for you to let God fully redeem that part of your story — not just forgive it, but actually use it?
6. Do you believe God can make your mess your message? What makes that hard to believe sometimes?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"My past does not own my identity. What was meant to destroy me, God is turning for my good. I am not what happened to me. I am not what I have done. I am what God says — redeemed, restored, and re-purposed. My story is not over. The best of it is still ahead."**

---

### Prayer Space

*Ask your mentee:*
- What wound or carry from your past do you want God to touch and redeem today?
- Is there anyone you need to forgive — yourself, someone else — as part of walking free?

# WEEK 7 — You Are Not Alone — You Are Part of Something Bigger
## Theme: Community — Identity is also corporate, not just personal

### Memory Verse
> *"Just as a body, though one, has many parts, but all its many parts form one body, so it is with Christ."*
> — **1 Corinthians 12:12**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
The western world has made identity hyper-individual. But biblical identity is also communal. One Peter 2:9 does not say *"you are a chosen individual"* — it says *"you are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation."*

This week, help your mentee see that their identity includes belonging to a body. How they show up, serve, and connect in community is an expression of that identity — not a distraction from it.

Also: celebrate. Week 7 is a good time to affirm the growth you have seen in your mentee since week 1. Be specific. Speak life.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. How has this program — your mentor relationship and accountability squad — impacted how you see yourself over the last 7 weeks?
2. Who are the people in your life right now that you feel called to walk with and serve? Who are your people?
3. What role did community play in your Bible character's journey? Did they need others to fulfill their calling?
4. Is there a moment where your character poured into others or carried others through a difficult season? What made that possible?
5. What do you uniquely bring to the community — church, family, friend group — that only you can bring?
6. How do you want to show up differently for the people around you as a result of this program?

---

### Weekly Proclamation

> **"I am not alone. I am part of a body, a generation, a movement that God is raising up. My identity is mine — and it is also bigger than me. I choose to show up, serve, pour out, and be present for the people God has placed in my world. I am not a spectator. I am a participant in what God is building."**

---

### Prayer Space

- Who in your circle — family, friend, church — needs prayer right now? Can we pray for them together?

# WEEK 8 — This Is Who You Are — Now Walk in It
## Theme: Commission — Identity received must be identity lived

### Memory Verse
> *"But you are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that you may proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of darkness into His marvelous light."*
> — **1 Peter 2:9**

---

### Mentor Exhortation
You made it to week 8. Celebrate. This is not the end of the journey — it is the beginning of a new way of walking.

This final week is about **commissioning** your mentee. Not a graduation into a title, but a sending into daily life with a settled identity. The same verse that opened this program closes it — but now your mentee has 8 weeks of Bible character study, scripture, prayer, and honest conversation behind them. The verse should land differently now.

Speak this week as a father or mother in faith. Affirm. Declare. Send.

---

### Conversation Questions

1. Who did you think you were when you started this program? Who do you know you are now?
2. Looking back at the entire journey of your Bible character — what is the one thing their life says about identity in God that you will carry with you?
3. What is the most significant shift in how you see yourself that happened during these 8 weeks?
4. What is one thing God showed you about yourself that surprised you?
5. What scripture from this program do you want to anchor your life to going forward?
6. What does it look like for you — practically, specifically — to *proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of darkness into light* in your school, your home, and your community?

---

### Mentor Commissioning — Speak This Over Your Mentee
*Read this aloud into their life as a spiritual declaration:*

> **"[Mentee's name], I have watched you over these 8 weeks, and I want to tell you what I see.**
> I see someone chosen by God — not because of your performance, but because of His love.
> I see someone designed on purpose, with gifts and a story that only you carry.
> I see someone called out of darkness into light — and I believe you are already beginning to shine.
> You are not who others said you were. You are not your past, your failures, or your fears.
> You are what God says: chosen, royal, holy, and His.
> Now go. Walk in it. We are watching, we are praying, and we are with you."**

---

### Weekly Proclamation — Final Declaration
*Speak this together aloud:*

> **"I am chosen. I am royal. I am holy. I belong to God. I was made on purpose, for a purpose, with a design that cannot be duplicated. My past is redeemed. My community is my strength. My future is in the hands of the One who called me out of darkness.**
> **I am not who the world says I am.**
> **I am who God says I am.**
> **And I will spend the rest of my life walking in that light."**
