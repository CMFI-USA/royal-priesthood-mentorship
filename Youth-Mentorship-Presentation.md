# Miracle Center Youth Mentorship Program
## Church + Leadership Presentation

Theme Verse: **1 Peter 2:9**

"But you are a chosen generation, a royal priesthood, a holy nation, His own special people, that you may proclaim the praises of Him who called you out of darkness into His marvelous light."

---

## Slide 1 - Title
**Miracle Center Youth Mentorship Program**  
**Dates:** April 19 - June 14 (8 weeks)  
**Audience:** Ages 13-25  
**Theme:** Identity in Christ (1 Peter 2:9)

Speaker note:
This program is a focused 8-week discipleship journey designed to help our youth and young adults know who they are in Christ and live confidently from that identity.

---

## Slide 2 - Why This Program, Why Now?
- Many youth are forming identity in a noisy culture
- Confusion about purpose, belonging, and value is increasing
- The church has a mandate to disciple the next generation
- This program creates intentional relationships and spiritual formation

Speaker note:
We are not just running an activity. We are building spiritual roots at a key life stage where identity choices shape destiny.

---

## Slide 3 - Biblical Foundation (1 Peter 2:9)
**Our youth are:**
- Chosen
- Royal priesthood
- Holy nation
- God's special people

**Our youth are called to:**
- Proclaim God's praises
- Live as people brought from darkness to light

Speaker note:
This verse gives both identity and assignment. We want youth to receive who they are and practice what they are called to do.

---

## Slide 4 - Program Goals
By the end of 8 weeks, youth should:
- Understand biblical identity in Christ
- Grow in prayer and scripture habits
- Build trusted relationships with mentors and peers
- Increase confidence in sharing faith and testimony
- Demonstrate spiritual maturity in daily life

---

## Slide 5 - Program Structure
- 1:1 mentor-mentee matching
- Weekly meeting (minimum 1 hour)
- Bible character study across 8 weeks
- Identity scripture memorization
- Prayer and bible-sharing rhythm
- Sunday message feedback discussion
- Accountability squad of 4 mentees
- Weekly accountability partner meeting

Speaker note:
The structure combines personal discipleship, scripture depth, and community accountability.

---

## Slide 6 - Key Dates and Flow
- **Program start:** Sunday, April 19
- **Midpoint mentor gathering:** Week 4 — week of May 11
- **Program end celebration:** Sunday, June 14 (after church)
- **Duration:** 8 weeks

Weekly rhythm:
1. Scripture + identity focus
2. Life check-in + prayer
3. Sunday message reflection
4. Next-step commitment

## Slide 8 - 8-Week Journey Overview
*Each week builds one layer of identity — from origin to commission.*

| Week | Theme | Key Truth | Memory Verse |
|------|-------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Origin | God knew you before the world named you | Jeremiah 1:5 |
| 2 | Design | You were crafted with intention | Psalm 139:13-14 |
| 3 | Belonging | You are a child of God — not earned, lavished | 1 John 3:1 |
| 4 | Purpose | Identity leads to assignment | Ephesians 2:10 |
| 5 | Character | Private holiness shapes public testimony | Romans 12:2 |
| 6 | Redemption | Your past does not have the final word | Romans 8:28 |
| 7 | Community | Identity is personal and corporate | 1 Corinthians 12:12 |
| 8 | Commission | Now walk in it | 1 Peter 2:9 |

Speaker note:
Each week, mentors guide their mentee through the lens of a Bible character the mentee personally chose to study. The questions and scripture connect the character's journey to the mentee's own identity in Christ.

## Slide 8 - Spiritual Growth Outcomes for Youth
Expected spiritual growth:
- Stronger identity in Christ over labels from culture
- Deeper prayer life and dependence on God
- Greater consistency in bible reading and memorization
- Healthier choices from renewed thinking
- Improved ability to hear, obey, and represent Christ

Possible testimonies we expect:
- Youth sharing personal transformation
- Reconciliation in relationships
- Growth in purity, discipline, and service

---

## Slide 9 - Benefits to the Church
Church-wide impact:
- Strengthens discipleship culture, not just attendance
- Builds leadership pipeline from youth to future ministry workers
- Increases intergenerational unity through mentoring relationships
- Encourages spiritual health in families through youth transformation
- Creates a repeatable model for future cohorts

Leadership value:
- Clear structure, checkpoints, and accountability
- Measurable growth indicators and testimonies
- Alignment with church mission to make disciples

---

## Slide 10 - Benefits for Parents (of Teens and Young Adults)
Parents are key partners in discipleship.

Benefits for families:
- Better communication between parents and youth
- Youth equipped with godly identity and decision-making
- Supportive mentor network around each young person
- Reinforced spiritual practices at home
- Increased hope and confidence for children's spiritual future

How parents can participate:
- Pray weekly for their child and mentor
- Ask reflection questions at home
- Encourage attendance and accountability
- Celebrate growth milestones
